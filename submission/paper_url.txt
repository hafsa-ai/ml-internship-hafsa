 1. Title + Abstract
Title:

Identifying High-Opportunity Web Pages Using Search Performance Data

Abstract:

This study analyzes search performance data to identify underperforming web pages with high growth potential. We develop an opportunity scoring model using key metrics such as Click-Through Rate (CTR), impressions, and traffic trends. Our results show that pages with high impressions but low CTR represent the most valuable optimization opportunities. The proposed model outperforms simple ranking-based approaches by prioritizing actionable insights. Based on findings, we recommend targeted content updates, title optimization, and continuous monitoring to improve performance.

📌 2. Problem

Websites often contain hundreds or thousands of pages, but not all contribute equally to traffic or engagement.

Key Question: Which pages should be updated to improve overall performance?

Challenges:

Raw metrics don’t directly show opportunity

⚙️ 4. Methodology
Features Used:
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
Impressions
Trend (growth/decline over time)
Opportunity Score Formula:

We define an opportunity score as:

Opportunity Score = Impressions × (1 − CTR)

Explanation:

High impressions = high visibility
Low CTR = underperformance
Combined = strong optimization opportunity
Classification Logic:
High Opportunity: High impressions + Low CTR
Low Opportunity: Low impressions or High CTR
Growing Pages: Increasing impressions over time
Declining Pages: Decreasing impressions
Baseline Model:
Rank pages by impressions only
Validation Method:
Compare top pages selected by:
Baseline (Impressions)
Proposed Model (Opportunity Score)
Evaluate which method highlights more actionable pages
